2. Definitions

  • Platform: The web environment where companies and hackers can register, collaborate, and report vulnerabilities.
  • Hacker (Security User): A registered person who seeks and reports web vulnerabilities through the Platform.
  • Company: A registered organization that uses the Platform to identify and correct vulnerabilities in its systems.
  • AI Agent: A specialized artificial intelligence model based on GPT, fine-tuned to assist hackers during the bug bounty process.
  • Triage: The process of verifying, validating, and prioritizing reported vulnerabilities before rewarding.
  • Reward: Payment granted to the hacker after the validation of a vulnerability.

3. Services

6xargs offers a platform where:

  • Companies can register for free and define the scope of their bug bounty programs through their own URLs.
  • Hackers can register for free and report web vulnerabilities ethically, following the triage process.
  • Hackers can interact with an integrated AI Agent to:
    • Automate repetitive processes.
    • Analyze vulnerabilities faster.
    • Create vulnerability reports efficiently.
  • Payments and rewards are processed through Stripe to ensure security and regulatory compliance.

4. Community Member Submissions

  • Hackers may submit vulnerability reports through the Platform.
  • Reports will be reviewed through the triage process to validate the existence, impact, and criticality of the vulnerability.
  • 6xargs does not guarantee the acceptance of all submitted reports, as the final decision will be made after the validation process.
  • Hackers agree that submitted reports may be used by companies to improve their security.
